\begin{block}{Research Objective}
  Rocket launch rates are rising, making sustainable recovery of payloads and first
  stages increasingly important. Autonomous surface vehicles (ASVs) can reduce cost,
  human exposure, and environmental footprint, but require real-time prediction of
  the rocket landing location. This work focuses on impact point prediction (IPP),
  which continuously estimates the landing location and uncertainty from onboard
  sensor data. The ASV uses this estimate for recovery guidance.

\begin{block}{IPP Architecture}

  \textbf{Navigation filter.} The ESKF estimates position, velocity, attitude, and
  sensor biases from inertial propagation with GNSS, barometer, and magnetometer
  aiding. Its state and covariance initialise the IPP filter.

  \vspace{0.8em}
  \textbf{Impact point prediction filter.} The IPP filter propagates a Cartesian state
  $\mathbf{x} = [\mathbf{p}^\top, \mathbf{v}^\top]^\top$ in NED coordinates forward
  until altitude reaches sea level:

  \vspace{0.2em}
  \begin{center}
  \begin{minipage}{0.97\columnwidth}
  \begin{equation*}
    \dot{\mathbf{p}} = \mathbf{v}, \qquad
    \dot{\mathbf{v}} = \mathbf{g}
      - K_D\,\mathbf{v}_a
      + \mathbf{R}(\hat{\mathbf{q}})\,\mathbf{a}_{T,b}, \qquad
    K_D = \frac{\rho\, C_D\, A\,\|\mathbf{v}_a\|}{2m}
  \end{equation*}
  \end{minipage}
  \end{center}

  \vspace{0.2em}
  $\mathbf{v}_a$ airspeed, $\rho$ air density, $C_D$ drag coefficient, $A$ reference area, $m$ mass.
  $\mathbf{a}_{T,b}$ body-frame thrust acceleration, rotated to NED by $\mathbf{R}(\hat{\mathbf{q}})$.

\end{block}

\begin{block}{Simulation Performance}
  The Cartesian IPP architecture was evaluated on four simulated trajectories using
  100 Monte Carlo wind realizations per trajectory. The vehicle model was kept close
  to truth, giving a controlled reference without the hardware saturation seen in
  flight data.
  
  \vspace{0.5em}
  The model achieved 45.3\,m mean RMSE across the four trajectories. The plots show
  median error and 3-sigma covariance.

\begin{block}{Discussion}
  \begin{minipage}[t]{0.50\columnwidth}
    \vspace{0pt}
    \raggedright
    Simulation results show accurate IPP under controlled model conditions, but
    real flight exposes practical limits. The dominant issue was gyroscope
    saturation during burn, visible as the 40\,rad/s ceiling in the spin-rate
    plot.
  \end{minipage}\hfill
  \begin{minipage}[t]{0.46\columnwidth}
    \vspace{0pt}
    \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/poster_spin_rate.pdf}
  \end{minipage}

  \vspace{0.5em}
  Beyond sensor saturation, uncertainty in wind, drag, mass, and thrust
  parameters limits prediction accuracy. Better wind estimation, drag
  identification, in-flight parameter estimation, and uncertainty quantification
  are key next steps.
\end{block}
